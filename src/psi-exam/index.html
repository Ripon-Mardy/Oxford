UKVI Approved English Proficiency Language Test

UKVI Approved English Proficiency Language Test – Simple, Quick, Affordable
Skills For English is now available in 95 countries around the world, and Morocco is one of them.

Why Skills for English?

Skills for English is a qualification that enables you to demonstrate your knowledge of the English language for many purposes, including work, study, or settlement. With Skills for English, you get:

  • A degree that has been verified by the Scottish Credentials Authority (SQA), a global innovator in the establishment of credentials.
  • A clear path for rapid progress toward your goal, with practical support, study aids, and practice tests
  • A variety of test levels to match your needs precisely, with the outcome based on a simple pass-mark
  • Simple on-demand booking and a choice of delivery channels and locations
  • A one-session test that is entirely computer-based with no examiner/assessor present
  • Auto-marked reading and listening skills, with speaking and writing components marked by expert assessors who do not see any of your details
  • A quick turn-around on marking with results being available in as little as 14 days

Levels tailored to your needs

Skills for English provides an accurate measure of language proficiency through practical and realistic tasks which you would expect to undertake in an English-speaking environment.

  • You get to choose the test level you require, from beginner to proficiency, for either two skills, speaking and listening, or four skills, speaking, listening, reading, and writing. That way, you know you are preparing for and taking precisely the level of test you need for your chosen purpose – no more, no less.

Computer-based testing for all skills

All Skills for English tests use computer-based delivery for all skills. Reading and listening are automatically marked during the test session itself; speaking and writing are recorded digitally during the test session and marked by trained assessors (who have no visibility of your details) after you’ve finished. It follows that you can test all the components in succession in a single session and get your results in as little as 14 days.

Speaking

First, you have to introduce yourself. Then you will listen to a series of recordings and respond to recorded questions and tasks that will be manifested on the screen. You will do this by registering your spoken answers.

Reading

The student will see a series of texts of varying lengths on screen. Then they will be asked multiple questions about them in different formats. These include multiple-choice, true/false/not given, answer matching, or written responses.

Listening

The student will listen to several recordings of spoken English. These will feature one or more speakers. After each, the student should answer a series of multiple-choice or short-word-answer questions.

Writing

The examiner will give the candidate two tasks, which may be on any relevant topic. Thus, they have to organize and complete an extended written response for each part. The candidate has to address the task and include the main keywords of the topic.

When you receive your results, you will be given details of the individual outcome (pass/fail) for each skill taken, plus your overall test outcome (also pass/fail): you need to pass each of the skills taken to pass the test overall.

Simple to take, simple to book

Skills for English language tests can be taken at one of our worldwide networks of PSI Authorized Test Centres, you can book on-demand all year round and take the test at a time and place that’s convenient to you.

UKVI SELT Exam: SKILLS FOR ENGLISH

Introduction

Skills for English is a Secure English Language Test (SELT). The UK Home Office for UK Visas and Immigration requires a pass in Skills for English to demonstrate your English language skills for your visa application. It is a computer-based exam since the student can take it at the Institute of Career Development, which is authorized by the  PSI Test Centre in Morocco.

Skills for English UKVI is equivalent to IELTS (UKVI) and objectively assesses test takers’ English language proficiency in four facets; listening, speaking, reading, and writing. The UK home office approves the exam Skills for English UKVI. Thus, one can use this exam to apply for a UK visa for study, work, and immigration.

Skills for English UKVI adopts a Pass/No Pass scoring method. Applicants have to pass each part of the test to achieve the final test score. In the past, applicants may have needed to take IELTS multiple times to obtain a high enough passing grade. By taking Skills for English UKVI, test takers only need to get a “Pass” result, which saves time and money during the visa application process.

Why Skills for English?

Skills for English is a qualification that enables you to demonstrate your knowledge of the English language for many reasons, including work, study, or settlement. With Skills for English, you get:

  • The candidate will get a qualification guaranteed by the Scottish Qualifications Authority (SQA). It is a global leader in education and qualification development.
  • The candidate will be given a variety of exam levels to best suit his demands.
  • A clear path for rapid progress toward your goal, with practical support, study aids, and practice tests
  • A one-session test. It is an entirely computer-based test, with no examiner/assessor present.
  • Auto-marked reading and listening skills, with speaking and writing components marked by expert assessors who do not see any of your details
  • Marking is completed quickly, with findings available in as little as 3 to 5 days.

Skills for English Exam Components:

The tests provide evidence of English language proficiency for many purposes, including work, study, and travel.

At levels A1, A2, and B1 (2 components: Speaking and Listening), test-takers demonstrate their ability to communicate verbally, respond to questions about themselves, and show their understanding of spoken English. The skills assessed include (but are not limited to):

  • responding to questions about themselves and their everyday context
  • engaging in functional and situational oral activities, such as giving an invitation
  • listening to a series of short texts and answering questions on what they hear

At levels B1, B2, C1, and C2 (4 components: Speaking, Listening, Reading, and Writing), test-takers demonstrate their ability to deal with more challenging contexts across all four skills. The skills assessed include (but are not limited to):

  • proficiency at relevant CEFR levels in writing, reading, listening, and speaking skills in English
  • understanding written and spoken texts in English in the context of everyday life
  • producing written English in the context of everyday life
  • applying knowledge and understanding of language in spoken and written English
